Evidence snapshot (UK)

Children/young people: structured, communication‑focused interventions, environmental adaptations and co‑occurring problem management; consider melatonin via specialists if behavioural sleep approaches fail. Sources: NICE CG170 (support & management). Educational only; not medical advice.

Adults: adapted CBT for anxiety/depression, structured support plans, and workplace/healthcare reasonable adjustments. Sources: NICE autism guidance (adults). Educational only.

Downloads: ⬇ Parent Guide (PDF) One‑page profile template ⬇ Parent quick‑pack (ZIP) NICE CG170 NHS: Autism overview NAS: What is autism? AET: For parents

Top 10 parent strategies (home‑friendly)

Follow the child’s lead (NDBI style) — Join their play, copy/expand language, and reinforce attempts many times daily [1][2].
First–Then and visual schedules — Make steps visible (photos/icons); reduce uncertainty and ease transitions [1][3].
Functional Communication Training (FCT) — Teach a simple way to ask (help/more/break) that meets the same need as the behaviour [1][4].
ABC notes to spot patterns — Antecedent–Behaviour–Consequence; change the trigger and teach an easier ask [4].
Social stories & role‑play — Explain what will happen and what to do in short, positive stories; practise first [3].
Sensory supports toolkit — Headphones, movement breaks, deep‑pressure options if liked; create a low‑arousal corner [1][3].
Sleep hygiene routine — Consistent schedule, dim lights, screens off ≥1h; consider clinical input if persistent [1][3].
Choice‑making — Offer structured choices (A/B) to build communication and reduce conflict [4].
Emotion check‑ins — Simple scales (colours/numbers) paired with agreed calming tools; review weekly [2].
Plan big transitions — Preview visits, countdown calendars, photos/videos of new places; pack comfort tools [3].

References

  1. [1] NICE CG170 (under‑19s). Support & management recommendations — nice.org.uk/guidance/cg170
  2. [2] Education Endowment Foundation. Metacognition & self‑regulated learning — educationendowmentfoundation.org.uk

Early signs and getting help

Early signs (examples)

  • Differences in social interaction/play; limited eye contact or shared attention.
  • Language differences (late speech, echoing, unusual tone) or strong interests.
  • Sensory differences (noise/light/touch), routines and predictability needs.

Every child is different; list is not diagnostic. Discuss concerns with a clinician.

Where to start

  • Under‑5s: talk to your health visitor or GP; school‑age: speak with the SENCo or GP.
  • Ask about local autism assessment pathways and wait‑list supports.
  • Contact SENDIASS for independent advice; request reasonable adjustments at school/appointments.
Useful links: NHS: autism IPSEA (education law) SENDIASS SEND Code of Practice

Behaviour support at home

Use ABC to understand the function, then teach an easier way to ask (FCT) and adjust triggers.

Tools: ABC quick tracker Emotion check‑in Choice‑making

Tips

Describe one behaviour clearly (observable words).
Reduce triggers (noise/unclear steps/too big tasks).
Prompt a simple request (help/more/break) and reinforce fast.
Plan safety first; seek clinical advice for risk.

Sleep

Behavioural sleep routines first; ask a clinician about melatonin only if routines fail.

Tools: Sleep routine builder Sleep tools →
Fixed sleep/wake times (±30 min).
Low light; screens off ≥1h; calming steps.
Track latency; adjust one thing at a time.
Discuss persistent problems with a clinician.

Sensory and regulation

Noise: headphones; quiet corner; visual cues.
Movement: walks, trampoline, heavy work.
Deep pressure: weighted lap if liked; stop if disliked.
Co‑regulate: breathe slowly together; use 2‑minute resets.
Try: 🟩 Box Breathing — 2 min ⚡ 60‑second Reset

School partnership (SEN support, EHCP)

  • Meet the SENCo; agree classroom adjustments and targets; share one‑page profile.
  • Monitor progress (“Assess‑Plan‑Do‑Review”); consider requesting an EHCP if needs are significant.
  • Plan transitions with visits, visuals and key‑adult introductions.
Resources: Teacher quick pack One‑page profile template Gov.uk: SEN support EHCP overview

Transitions and adulthood

  • Key transitions: nursery→primary, primary→secondary, school→college/employment.
  • Use visual countdowns, photos/videos, and practice runs with a key adult.
  • Post‑16: ask about Access to Work, reasonable adjustments, and local supported internships.
Links: Access to Work NAS: transitions

Parent & carer wellbeing

  • Protect sleep and respite; use short breaks where available.
  • Join local support groups (school, NHS, charities) for shared tips and community.
  • Seek your GP’s help for stress/anxiety; ask about carer’s assessments.
Support: Carers UK NHS: support for carers

Children & Adolescents

  • Naturalistic, caregiver‑mediated coaching (NDBI style): 3–5 min play bursts, copy/expand/celebrate, many times daily.
  • AAC is speech‑friendly: pictures/PECS/core boards/apps reduce frustration; keep AAC available everywhere.
  • Visual & structured teaching: First/Then, visual schedules, clear work systems; teach in small steps.
  • Behaviour → ABC: track antecedent–behaviour–consequence; teach an easier way to ask; reinforce quickly.
  • Sleep: regularity, routine, light/noise/temperature; discuss melatonin only with a clinician if insomnia persists.

Adults

  • Employment: reasonable adjustments (Equality Act), clear written instructions, low‑sensory spaces, flexible hours/hybrid.
  • Access to Work: workplace assessment, job coach, assistive tech, travel support.
  • Mental health: adapted CBT (visual structure, concrete goals, graded exposure with sensory planning); monitor medication with clinicians.
  • Regulation: micro‑breaks, noise management, meltdown/shutdown plans.

Later Life

  • Anticipate sensory/hearing/vision changes; schedule quieter appointments.
  • Use pill organisers, checklists, and carer prompts; prepare hospital passports.
  • Ask the local authority about adult social care and carers’ assessments.

Neurodevelopmental: ADHD (quick pointers)

  • Core features: inattention, impulsivity, hyperactivity across settings.
  • Supports: behavioural strategies, executive‑function scaffolds, environmental cues; review sleep, anxiety.

Mental Health: Anxiety • Depression • Stress • Sleep

  • CBT (adapted): visual agendas, concrete skills practice, stepwise exposure with sensory planning.
  • Sleep: consistent timing, low light, screens off ≥1h; record latency; escalate to clinician if persistent.

Breathing & Focus (2‑minute reset)

  1. Sit comfortably; relax shoulders and jaw.
  2. Inhale quietly through the nose ~4 s; exhale ~6 s.
  3. Repeat 10 cycles; notice shoulders lowering and heart rate easing.

Use before transitions, meetings or bedtime wind‑down.
