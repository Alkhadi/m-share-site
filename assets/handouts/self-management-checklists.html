Self‑Management Checklists

Educational information; not medical advice.

What it is: Pupils use a simple list or visual to tick off steps and rate effort/feeling, building independence and on‑task behaviour.

Why it helps: Makes expectations concrete, supports metacognition, and fades adult prompts over time.

How to use

  1. Choose one routine (e.g., “start work”, “finish pack‑up”). Keep 3–5 steps.
  2. Co‑create the checklist with the pupil; add a tiny timer or token if motivating.
  3. Model once; then pupil self‑checks; adult spot‑checks and praises accuracy.
  4. Fade adult prompts; use brief “how did it go?” ratings to grow reflection.
  5. Review weekly; update steps as skills improve.

Checklist

  • Clear, visible steps; one routine at a time.
  • Teach, then hand over to pupil; praise honest self‑checks.
  • Link to small, immediate reinforcement when appropriate.
