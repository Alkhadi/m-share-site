Video Modelling & Prompting

Classroom evidence‑based technique. Educational information; not medical advice.

What it is: A short video shows the exact behaviour, language, or routine to copy (peer, adult, or self in the clip). The pupil watches and then practises immediately, pausing the video at key steps for brief prompts.

Why it helps: Clear visual examples reduce ambiguity and support memory; modelling and immediate practice improve generalisation to similar tasks.

How to use (quick steps)

  1. Pick one target (e.g., greeting, lining up, turn‑taking, asking for help).
  2. Record a 15–45 s clip of the steps or language (peer/adult; keep background simple).
  3. Watch once. Rewatch with pauses at key points; prompt with 1–3 words or a gesture.
  4. Practise the same steps in class; reinforce attempts; repeat daily for a week.
  5. Fade video use as success grows; introduce a similar context to generalise.

Checklist

  • One target only; short script; quiet visuals.
  • Pause at the hardest step; prompt briefly; then pause less over time.
  • Celebrate attempts (verbal/token) and track a quick success count.
